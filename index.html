. current research

I'm currently working on an industrial project, in collaboration with ACMI Spa. Focus of this project is to introduce Artificial Intelligence as a support for the packaging industry. Purposes are, but not limited to, enhance safety for workers, reduce plastic usage, optimize production lines, simplify and improve human-machine interfaces.

For this project, I've developed a simulation in Pychrono to retrieve dynamic information about pallet stability. The key challenge was to make the model adhere to physical tests. The work is summarized in the following papers:

2023
E. Iotti, A. Dal Palù, G. Contesso, F. Bertinelli: Substitute Plastic Film with Kraft Paper in Automatic Pallet Wrapping: An AI Pipeline.
2023
E. Iotti, A. Dal Palù, F. Bertinelli, G. Contesso: Towards Greener Logistics: a Multi-Body Simulation to Test Pallet Stability.

I'm advancing this idea by 3D modeling the shrink wrap around the pallet, using Blender. The next step is to change material, from-plastic-to-paper, and, again, make the virtual simulation adhere to reality.

. current teaching

I'm the course lecturer of Elements of Logic and Discrete Structures. That is a fundamental course at the first year of the Computer Science degree. Course topics are the basics of the mathematical language, a little bit of discrete mathematics, propositional logic and a taste of first-order logic.

I'm also the course lecturer of Foundations of Artificial Intelligence. That is a preparatory course about AI in general at the first year of the Computer Science master degree. Topics are, in order of presentation, the Python programming language from an AI developer point of view, the basics of differential calculus (gradients and so on...), some bits of ODEs, an introduction to computer vision and deep learning, a brief discussion about recurrent networks and language models, and many other little things that serve as context to understand later AI courses.

As a member of the Computer Science degree's board, I supervised some students bachelor's and master's theses (ctrl-F eleonora iotti). I'm also the advisor of many internship at UniPR.

. side projects

I'm investigating emerging phenomena in neural networks, in particular when logic and causal behaviors appear. The idea stemmed by the recent growing interest in explainable AI. Part of these emerging behaviors should have something to do with Horn-clauses, Hasse diagrams, and Huffman codes. However, this is yet a work-in-progress.

I'm also the coordinator of another project with ACMI Spa, which outcome is a software to manage the whole process of creating a packaging schema. AI is involved in retrieve best pallet layouts, and in finding the most efficient way of manipulating packages on machine belts. In this project are involved some intern students of the Computer Science degree, and two scholarship students of the Computer Science master degree.

I collaborate to the CARING project, which scope is to gain information about organs and possible anomalies in computed tomography scans. The approach we proposed is a geometric-based computer vision system with sub-voxel accuracy. This finding is presented in the following paper:

2022
F. Bertini, A. Dal Palù, F. Fabiano, E. Iotti: CARING for xAI

Future expected developments involve the use of ontologies and logic programming to provide feedback to medical doctors.

. previous research

I used to work on JADE multi-agent system framework, by developing a novel programming language, initially called JADEL. Focus of my work was to explore the capabilities of an imperative agent-oriented language, wrt the usual declarative ones. Another main challenge was to formalize its semantics. I've done most of my PhD research on that subject, and below there's a list of related publications. Authors are most of the time in alphabetical order.

. education

I have a PhD in Information Technologies at UniPR, bestowed on 02/03/2018. My thesis is entitled An Agent-Oriented Programming Language for JADE Multi-Agent Systems and, as you may expect, focuses on the design and development of JADEL.

I graduated in Mathematics in 2014, with a dissertation on random graphs sequences, studying their behaviors and convergence conditions. Before, I graduated with a bachelor's degree in Mathematics and Computer Science in 2011, presenting a survey study about probabilistic ramification processes.

In 2008, I obtained a Secondary School Diploma in Information Technologies, discussing the use of Laplace transforms for simple electronic devices.

My grades were almost always excellent.
